Landscape User Guide

Landscape is a category intelligence tool for analyzing Seima products against competitors. It has four views, each designed for a different level of analysis.

Getting Started

When you open Landscape, it loads the Seima product catalogue and all competitor data (products and matches). This takes a few seconds — you'll see a progress bar in the top right.

Competitor chips appear in the toolbar below the navbar. All competitors are active by default. Click a chip to toggle it off (it dims). Click again to bring it back. At least one competitor must always be active. The active competitors affect all four views.

View 1: Matrix

A grid where rows are product categories (Basins, Tapware, Toilets, etc.) and columns are brands (Seima + your selected competitors). Each cell shows product counts and match status.

How to read a cell

  • The large number is the total product count for that brand in that category
  • Below it: 8✓ 5⏳ 15— means 8 verified matches, 5 AI-suggested matches waiting for review, 15 with no match
  • The thin colored bar shows verification progress (green = verified, amber = pending)

Coverage column

The far-right column shows Seima's product count as a percentage of the largest competitor's count in that row. Green = 80%+, amber = 50–79%, red = below 50%.

Toggle modes (top right)

  • Count — default, product counts with match status
  • Coverage — counts with verification percentage
  • Avg Price — average price per cell with product count

Drill down

Click any cell to jump to the Gap Analysis view, pre-filtered to that category and competitor.

What to look for

  • Red coverage percentages — categories where competitors significantly out-range you
  • Cells with many pending (⏳) matches — validation work that needs doing
  • Categories where Seima has products but competitors show 0 — your unique strengths

View 2: Gaps

A side-by-side comparison of Seima products versus one or more competitors within a single category. Seima products are on the left rail, competitor products on the right.

Getting there

Either click a cell in the Matrix, or select a category from the dropdown in the toolbar.

Summary stats

The header shows: total Seima products, matched count, unique count (no competitor equivalent), and gap count (competitor products with no Seima equivalent).

Product card badges

  • Matched (green) — this Seima product has a verified competitor match
  • Unique (blue) — no competitor matches this product. This is our differentiator
  • Gap (red) — this competitor product has no Seima match. This is an opportunity
  • AI (amber) — AI has suggested a match but it hasn't been verified yet

Price comparison

For matched products, you'll see: Seima: $389 (+12%) — meaning Seima is 12% more expensive than the competitor product.

Inline validation

AI-suggested matches show Verify and Reject buttons. Click to validate without leaving the page. The view refreshes automatically.

What to look for

  • Red "Gap" badges on competitor products — products they offer that we don't
  • Blue "Unique" badges on Seima products — our competitive advantages
  • Price comparison trends — are we consistently over or under-priced?
  • Amber AI matches that need verification

View 3: Constellation

A force-directed graph where every product is a dot and matched products are connected by lines. Products cluster by category naturally.

Reading the graph

  • Each dot is a product. Colour = brand (Seima is copper/gold; competitors have distinct colours shown in the legend)
  • Lines between dots = matches. Solid green = verified. Dashed amber = AI-suggested
  • The physics simulation pulls matched products together and pushes unmatched ones apart
  • Category labels appear as large faint text in the background

Interactions

  • Zoom — scroll wheel or +/− buttons (top right)
  • Pan — click and drag empty space
  • Drag a node — reposition it (it springs back when released)
  • Hover over a dot to see product details (image, name, brand, code, price)
  • Filter by category — use the dropdown in the toolbar

What to look for

  • Dense clusters with mixed brand colours = heavy competition
  • Isolated competitor dots with no nearby Seima dots = white space (gaps)
  • Seima dots surrounded by competitor colours = well-covered categories
  • Connected dots far apart = potentially bad cross-category matches

View 4: Canvas

A freeform workspace where you create named groups and drag products into them. It's a whiteboard for category planning sessions.

Product tray (left sidebar)

  • Search products by name or code
  • Filter by brand using the filter chips
  • Products show a small brand-coloured dot for identification

Creating groups

  • Click + New Group in the toolbar or the floating bar
  • Or drag a product from the tray onto empty workspace — a new group is created automatically at the drop position

Working with groups

  • Rename — click the group name and type
  • Move — drag the group header bar
  • Add products — drag from the tray or from another group
  • Remove a product — double-click it
  • Delete a group — click the × button in its header

Saving

Click Save in the toolbar or floating bar. Groupings are stored in your browser (device-specific) and persist across sessions.

Example uses

  • "Good / Better / Best" price tier analysis
  • "Keep / Discontinue / Gap to Fill" for range review
  • "Builder Standard / Designer Premium" customer segmentation
  • "Needs Review" staging area for products to validate later

Tips

  • Start with the Matrix to get the big picture, then drill into categories that need attention
  • Use Gaps to validate — it's faster than the standalone Validator because you see matches in category context
  • The Constellation is ideal for presentations — it turns abstract data into something visual
  • Canvas groupings don't affect actual data — they're your personal workspace for thinking and planning
